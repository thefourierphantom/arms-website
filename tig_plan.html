12
Orbital internship: learn IPC-A-620 by week 3 IPC-A-620 is the workmanship standard for wire harness and cable assembly. It is what Orbital builds to. Go beyond just doing your job — understand why each standard exists and what failure mode it prevents. By week 3 you should be able to explain IPC-A-620 to someone who has never heard of it. This becomes TIG's first workshop topic: "Building to Defense Workmanship Standards." Most engineering students have never heard of this standard. TIG members will know it before their first internship interview.
Why: The curriculum you build for TIG comes directly from what you learn at Orbital. IPC-A-620 is the first piece of that curriculum being written in real time.
13
Orbital internship: document one new skill per week without exception Every week, one entry in your TIG intelligence file under "Curriculum Material": skill name, what it is, why it matters in defense manufacturing, what happens when you get it wrong. These entries are TIG's workshop curriculum being written by someone with real manufacturing floor experience. Nobody else building a defense engineering student org has this.
Why: Your curriculum has to be grounded in what defense contractors actually do. Your weekly documentation IS the curriculum research.

Why: Track 2 is communications and signals. You need RF foundations before Wireless Circuits and Radar Systems hit in your coursework.
