// OPERATIONAL BRIEF

ARMS is a student-led defense systems organization built around applied research, prototyping, testing, and documentation. Its purpose is to give technically serious students a place to work on real, defense-relevant engineering problems before industry, graduate research, or national security institutions give them that opportunity.

Students interested in defense, military systems, national security technology, autonomy, communications, sensing, and field-ready engineering often have no clear place to do serious applied work. Most student organizations are too broad, too social, or too unfocused to produce meaningful technical outcomes.

ARMS exists to close that gap. We give technically serious students a structured environment to design, prototype, test, and document applied engineering work connected to real military-relevant problems. ARMS is built for students who want to become more capable in the kinds of systems work that matter in defense and national security environments.

02 // OPERATIONAL DOMAINS
PROJECT
AREAS
DOMAIN 01
SENSING & ISR
Remote sensing field nodes, detection systems, ISR-support platforms, and field data acquisition tools that provide situational awareness in contested environments.
DOMAIN 02
COMMUNICATIONS
Resilient comms nodes, portable relay systems, RF link design, and signal propagation architectures for denied or degraded network environments.
DOMAIN 03
AUTONOMOUS SYSTEMS
Robotic support platforms, teleoperated scouts, UAS applications, and autonomous coordination systems built around real operational constraints and requirements.
DOMAIN 04
EMBEDDED SYSTEMS
Firmware development, microcontroller programming, real-time systems design, and hardware-software integration for defense platform applications.
DOMAIN 05
POWER SYSTEMS
Power management modules for field systems, energy architecture for unmanned platforms, power distribution design, and field-deployable power solutions.
